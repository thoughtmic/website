Messaging System

Locked 2026-05-20 · Branch feat/messaging-system-doc · Source of truth for the homepage, /for-ai-work/, /values/, onboarding cards, blog, outreach.
Why this doc exists. If thoughtmic.com, onboarding cards (Pxc), blog posts, and outreach DMs each describe ThoughtMic slightly differently, the result feels like multiple products. This doc defines the core promise once in four lengths so every surface stays consistent. Edit one length, you know which others to update.

Summary

The product has one promise stated four ways. Short forms compress; long forms expand. All four are derivatives of the same source idea: spoken thoughts become structured, connected memory in your vault.

  • Tagline (primary, brand mark): 7 words. OG cards, footer signature, app icon page.
  • Supporting taglines (functional + pain hook): for evaluation surfaces and attention-grabbing copy.
  • Sub-hero: 18 words. Homepage and /for-ai-work/ hero.
  • Secondary depth line: 15 words. Below sub-hero. Unpacks what "structured note" means.
  • Medium form: ~50 words. App store, blog opener, About-style sections.
  • Long form: ~90 words. /for-ai-work/ page body, deep-dive blog posts.

One source, four lengths

Every surface picks the right length for its space and audience. The taglines do brand-level work. The sub-hero and depth line do homepage hero work. The medium and long forms do explanation work. None contradict each other. They scale the same promise.

When this messaging changes in the future, edit the locked copy in this doc first. Then propagate to the surfaces that reference it. Never edit a surface in isolation.

Primary tagline Locked

T-A

7 words · use everywhere ThoughtMic appears as a brand mark

Make your spoken thoughts your memory.

Imperative verb (Make) signals there's a product doing work. The framing positions spoken thought as memory material, which is the actual product thesis. Calm, declarative, slightly philosophical. Works at every size without losing meaning. Doesn't pin us to a specific feature, so we can change implementation without changing brand.

Supporting taglines Locked

Two supporting taglines exist for specific surfaces where T-A doesn't do the right job. Both are locked. Neither replaces T-A as the brand tagline.

T-B Functional descriptor

6 words · use where the visitor needs to know what the product does in an evaluation context

Turn spoken thoughts into searchable memory.

Names input ("spoken thoughts"), output ("searchable memory"), and action ("turn into"). Reads as product description. Lives on app store subtitle, /for-ai-work/ intro, comparison pages (/vs/wispr-flow/), blog author bylines.

T-E Pain hook

7 words · use where you need to grab attention from someone who hasn't decided to care yet

Catch what you say before you forget.

Direct pain framing. Reads like advice from a friend rather than a brand promise. Lives in paid ad headlines, outreach DM openers, blog post titles, email subject lines. Slightly more informal than T-A; not for hero-level brand placement.

Sub-hero Locked

The dual-output promise

18 words · sits below the hero H1 on thoughtmic.com and on /for-ai-work/

Capture thoughts without typing. Your words land at your cursor, and a structured note lands in your vault.

Two sentences. Benefit-first opener ("without typing") resonates with the audience pain of typing fatigue. Parallel "land at" / "land in" carries the dual output without spelling it out as "two places." No habit claims, no competitor names, no AI-bland phrases. The shortcut ⌥ Space deliberately lives elsewhere on the page (see Shortcut placement) so the sub-hero stays focused on PROMISE not implementation.

Secondary depth line Locked

D1

15 words · sits directly below the sub-hero, smaller weight

Auto-titled, tagged, and connected to thoughts you've already saved with [[wikilinks]]. Your graph grows itself.

Unpacks what "structured note" means in one beat. The [[wikilinks]] glyph is brand-distinctive (mirrors the locked logo brackets) and Obsidian users get the syntax instantly. "Connected to thoughts you've already saved" makes the existing-graph value explicit. "Your graph grows itself" echoes the existing pillar #3 copy on the live homepage, so this line REINFORCES rather than DUPLICATES the page below. See visual treatment for sizing and weight.

Medium form Locked

~50 words

App store subtitle area, About page intro, blog opener, social bio

Capture thoughts without typing. Your words land at your cursor, and a structured note lands in your vault. Auto-titled, tagged, and connected to thoughts you've already saved with [[wikilinks]]. The vault becomes searchable memory you can query from Claude, Cursor, or any MCP client.

First two sentences are the locked sub-hero verbatim. Third sentence is the locked D1 depth line verbatim. Fourth sentence is the bridge: explicitly names the AI-memory wedge and the MCP clients. 48 words total. Three derivations from locked source plus one new bridge line; no drift risk.

Long form Locked

~90 words

/for-ai-work/ page body, deep-dive blog posts, About page body

ThoughtMic is a local voice layer for your Markdown vault. Hold ⌥ Space anywhere on your Mac and speak. Your words appear where you're typing (IDE, Slack, email, browser) so you keep working at speed. At the same time, a structured Markdown note lands in your Obsidian vault: title generated from the thought, tags suggested from your existing library, wikilinks resolved against notes you already have. Your spoken thoughts become memory you can query from Claude, Cursor, or any MCP client. The graph grows itself.

First sentence categorizes the product (local voice layer). Second sentence introduces the shortcut explicitly because this is the only length where naming the shortcut is appropriate (deeper-funnel reader). Sentences 3 and 4 expand the dual-output promise with concrete examples of WHERE the cursor lands (IDE, Slack, email, browser) and WHAT the vault note actually contains (title, tags, wikilinks). Closing sentence makes the AI-memory wedge explicit. 86 words.

Hero H1 (locked, reference only)

The fastest path from spoken thought to a real note.

Currently live on thoughtmic.com. Not part of this messaging refresh. Stays locked. Mentioned here so the surface map is complete: hero H1 sits ABOVE the locked sub-hero on the homepage and on /for-ai-work/ (with a different H1:see surface map).

Shortcut placement (⌥ Space)

The sub-hero deliberately does not include ⌥ Space. The shortcut is implementation detail; the sub-hero is promise. The shortcut lives in three other places on the homepage:

  1. Hero deck card 01 label. Add ⌥ Space next to "You spoke" so the visual story includes it from card one.
  2. Microcopy strip below the sub-hero / D1. A small line: "Activates with ⌥ Space anywhere on your Mac." Lives in the same compat-strip area as the existing "Lands in Obsidian / Logseq" line. Same weight, same color.
  3. Demo video poster overlay. The keystroke gets a captioned overlay on the demo poster image.

In the long form copy (above), the shortcut IS named in sentence two. That's intentional: long form is for deeper-funnel readers who want to know "how do I use this." They get the answer there.

Visual treatment for the depth line (D1)

D1 sits directly below the sub-hero and ABOVE the existing hero deck cards. It is supporting detail, not a competing headline. Treatment:

  • Size. 16-18px on desktop. Smaller than sub-hero (~22-24px) but not microscopic.
  • Weight. Regular (400). Not bold.
  • Color. Muted text color (lower contrast than the sub-hero, e.g. --fg-muted).
  • Spacing. ~12-16px gap above (small distance from sub-hero, treated as a continuation). ~32-40px gap below (clear break before deck cards begin).
  • The [[wikilinks]] glyph. Render in monospace font with a subtle forest tint (--accent) and a very soft background (--accent-soft at ~40% opacity). This is the only place on the homepage where the bracket notation is visually styled. Reinforces brand without shouting.

Things we will NOT say

Negative guardrails. These apply across every surface: website, onboarding, blog, outreach, ads.

  • No em-dashes (—) or en-dashes (–). Use periods, commas, colons, parentheses, or two short sentences. Em-dashes read as an AI tell. Audit copy before merging.
  • No "AI-powered" / "AI-driven" / "powered by AI". If we have to lead with "AI" as a marketing adjective, we're a wrapper. We're a voice layer that uses AI as a utility. Say what we do, not what powers us.
  • No "revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "magical" / "delightful". Hollow superlatives. We claim what we deliver, not how it feels.
  • No naming competitors defensively. Wispr Flow, Superwhisper, Mem, Reflect. Comparison pages (/vs/*) name them once. Brand-level copy does not.
  • No cloud provider names. No Cerebras, Groq, Fireworks, OpenAI, Anthropic in user-facing copy outside the values page commitment #4 (which generically names "the cloud" without specifying the provider). Provider can change; brand cannot read like a provider press release.
  • No unvalidated habit claims. Do NOT say "your daily routine," "your weekly review habit," "every morning you...". Until real users have demonstrated those habits, they are hypotheses. The current live sub-hero copy violates this with "ready for inbox review"; P3 of the implementation work fixes this.
  • No promises about downstream graph value users will feel. "Discover patterns in your thinking." "Surface forgotten ideas." "Build connections you didn't know you had." All hypothesis until validated. Wait for v1.1 to write that copy from real user behavior.
  • No "simply" or "just". "Just press Option-Space." "Simply capture your thoughts." Minimizes the user's effort patronizingly.
  • No "second brain" as a primary descriptor. Tiago Forte's book and Obsidian's community already own the framing. We can REFERENCE it ("your second brain" in passing) but not LEAD with it.
  • No "transform your workflow" / "10x your productivity". Productivity-marketing slop.
  • No "We use AI to ___" framing. Inverts the agency. Better: "AI does ___ for you." User stays the subject.
